v1
- User is the dominant percussion source
- Recording is drum-only or drum-dominant
- Drumless backing tracks are allowed
- Full commercial songs with drums are out of scope
- Percussive hits produce detectable audio transients
- Onset detection is sufficient
- Instrument classification is unnecessary
- Tempo is relatively stable (static throughout)
- Time signature remains consistent
- Recording quality is reasonable (no extreme distortion, clipping, or noise)
- A single microphone/audio recording is sufficient
- Audio is the primary input modality
- MIDI support is deferred (although electric kits support MIDI, keeping consistent with the audio-only input modality is preferred
and to account for acoustic kits)
- Consistency is the core problem (how consistent is the drummer's performance with respect to timing)
- Objective timing feedback is more important than complete transcription
